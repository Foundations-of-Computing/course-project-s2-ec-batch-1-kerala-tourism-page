Munnar


Escape to Munnar, a serene hill station nestled in the heart of Kerala, where lush tea gardens, misty hills, and cool mountain air await. Explore breathtaking landscapes, vibrant wildlife, and the charm of colonial-era bungalows while hiking through tranquil trails or enjoying a peaceful boat ride. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or simply a break from the ordinary, Munnar offers an unforgettable retreat for every traveler.

Jadayu Para


Discover the majestic Jadayu Para in Kollam, a stunning rock formation steeped in legend and offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes. Nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Western Ghats, this ancient site is perfect for adventure seekers with its trekking paths and mythological significance linked to the epic Ramayana. Whether you're drawn to its natural beauty, rich history, or breathtaking vistas, Jadayu Para promises a unique experience for all who visit.

District : Kollam

Kuttanad

Kuttanad is a land of unparalleled natural beauty and cultural richness. This enchanting region, also known as the ‘Rice Bowl of Kerala’, is nestled in the heart of the state, bordered by the majestic Western Ghats mountain range to the East and the Arabian Sea to the West. Its unique topography, with its vast network of interconnected canals, lakes, and lagoons, sets it apart from any other place on earth, creating a mesmerizing tapestry of emerald green paddy fields, swaying palm trees, and sparkling backwaters.

District : Alappuzha

Rating :

Kumbalangi

Kumbalangi in Ernakulam district has come a long way in transforming itself from a tiny island to a model fishing village and thereby a popular tourist spot. Sea sparkle or bioluminescence, locally known as ‘kavaru’ is a unique attraction along the backwaters of Kumbalangi. The ‘magical glow’ is caused by the high concentration of a micro-plankton called Noctiluca Scintillans in the sea. Sea sparkle is likely to happen during the period after the rains, in the months of September and October.

District : Ernakulam

Wayanad

Wayanad, a verdant district nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is a picturesque destination that offers a harmonious blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and adventure. Known for its sprawling spice plantations, mist-covered mountains, and diverse wildlife, Wayanad provides an ideal retreat for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and thrill-seekers alike.

District : Wayanad

Kumarakom

A cluster of little islands on the picturesque Vembanad Lake, Kumarakom is a part of the Kuttanad region. With a labyrinth of backwaters and mangrove forests all around it, Kumarakom is a universe in itself. A laid-back, soothing world of its own. It was Mr. Alfred George Baker, his interest in agriculture and his love for nature that helped him create this treasure called Kumarakom from over 500 acres of wetlands that lay north-east of Lake Vembanad.

Payyoli

Step in to Payyoli Beach in Kozhikode district and get ready to be blown away by its golden sands, shallow waters, swaying palm trees and calm environs. Located around 35 km north of Kozhikode, the beach is ideal for swimming and other water sports.

District : Kozhikode

Kovalam

A beach town on the Arabian Sea in southern India, Kovalam features three spectacular crescent beaches. The most popular and largest beach is Lighthouse Beach, aptly named for its 30-metre lighthouse, which features a stunning site - the Vizhinjam mosque. For a less crowded beach, visit Samudra Beach, with ample coconut trees and great views of the local fishermen at work.

District : Trivandrum

Bekal

The unique Bekal Fort stands amidst the roaming but heartening music of the seas along with the eternal not of the unending waves, carrying the trilling memories of a bygone era. This foremost souvenir remains undeterred over the ages as a silent witness to centuries of eventful yesteryears.

Vadakkumnathan Temple

The renowned Vadakkumnathan temple is one of the oldest temples in Kerala. Situated in the heart of Thrissur town, it is admired for its unique architectural structure, made purely in wood and stone. The annual festival of this temple, ‘Thrissur Pooram’ is often referred to as the festival of festivals, because of its richness, vibrance, and magnitude.
